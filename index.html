01 / About

A chemist working where tradition meets discovery

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Chemistry at the University of Dhaka, where I teach organic and general chemistry and lead research in natural products. My work centers on discovering and validating the bioactive molecules that give medicinal plants their therapeutic power.

Trained in organic chemistry (MS, University of Dhaka), I combine hands-on isolation and structural elucidation with computational tools — molecular docking, structure–activity analysis, machine learning, and cheminformatics — to move systematically from a plant extract to a characterized, biologically active lead. Alongside the bench, I bring a strong programming and analytical background that supports data-driven, reproducible science.

I have authored six peer-reviewed publications, led three funded research projects, and mentored eighteen graduate and undergraduate researchers — and I care deeply about teaching that helps students think independently and fall in love with the science.

02 / Research

Research interests

My research sits at the intersection of natural products chemistry, phytochemistry, and computational, AI-driven drug discovery. I investigate medicinal plants and natural sources to isolate, characterize, and evaluate bioactive secondary metabolites — pairing classical phytochemical screening and advanced spectroscopic elucidation with molecular docking, machine learning, and in-vitro / in-vivo pharmacological assays. The goal is to translate traditional herbal knowledge into validated, biologically active molecules and evidence-based leads for modern therapeutics.

Emerging focus

Machine Learning & AI for Drug Discovery

Applying machine learning and QSAR/QSPR modeling to predict bioactivity, ADMET, and structure–property relationships — accelerating the discovery and ranking of natural-product leads through cheminformatics and data-driven virtual screening.

Natural Products & Phytochemistry

Isolation, purification, and structural elucidation of secondary metabolites and fatty acids from medicinal plants and natural bio-sources.

Bioactivity & Pharmacology

Antioxidant, cytotoxic, thrombolytic, analgesic, antidiabetic, antidepressant, and membrane-stabilizing evaluation through in-vitro and in-vivo assays.

Computational Drug Discovery

Molecular docking and structure–activity relationship studies with AutoDock Vina, PyRx, and Discovery Studio to prioritize and rationalize therapeutic leads.

Analytical Characterization

Structural identification and fatty-acid profiling using NMR, FT-IR, UV-Vis, HPLC, GC-FID, and XRD for rigorous, reproducible characterization.

Medicinal & Synthetic Chemistry

Design and synthesis of bioactive scaffolds — thiazolidinedione, dihydropyrimidine, and benzimidazole derivatives — guided by structure–activity insight.

Green Synthesis & Materials

Green-synthesized metal-oxide nanoparticles and valorization of bio-waste (chitin, chitosan, carboxymethyl chitosan) for biological and catalytic applications.

06 / Teaching

Teaching & mentorship

“To me, teaching is not just about explaining a subject or completing a syllabus. It is about helping students grow in confidence, think independently, and develop a genuine interest in learning — a classroom where they feel free to ask questions, share ideas, and learn from mistakes without fear of judgment.”

I treat students as active participants, connecting abstract chemistry to real situations through discussion, examples, and problem-solving. Because every student learns differently, I use varied methods and value respect, collaboration, and open communication — and I see assessment as a way to build confidence and ownership, not just assign grades.
